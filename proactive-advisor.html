Work

Proactive Advisor

Turning insight into confident action

Concept → leadership buy-in → fast-tracked to development
Role: Design Advisor & Experience Lead
Timeline: 2 weeks

The problem

Employers and brokers had limited visibility into how their employees were actually using the services they were paying for.

Data existed, but it wasn’t actionable. Trends, risks, and successes were buried in reports that required interpretation—slowing decision-making.

The opportunity wasn’t to show more data, but to surface what mattered—clearly, quickly, and with confidence.

My role

I served as the design advisor and experience lead—responsible for framing the problem, setting experience direction, guiding conceptual design, and shaping the narrative presented to leadership.

The design team executed the work. My role was to ensure clarity, confidence, and alignment at every step.

Constraints

  • Two-week conceptual timeline
  • High executive visibility
  • No appetite for speculative or exploratory work
  • Needed to feel credible enough to invest in immediately

Designing for decision-making

Rather than designing a dense analytics dashboard, we focused on decision clarity.

We asked:

  • What should someone understand in 30 seconds?
  • Which patterns matter more than raw numbers?
  • How do we surface risk without creating alarm?

The experience prioritized:

  • Signals over noise
  • Trends over snapshots
  • Plain-language framing over technical labels

Concept exploration

This work needed to change how leaders understood the problem—not just what they saw.

Outcome

The concept resonated immediately with leadership.

What began as a two-week conceptual effort was fast-tracked into development—based on the clarity of the story and confidence in the direction.

The project validated the premise that strong framing and narrative can accelerate product decisions as much as execution.

Reflection

This project reinforced the importance of designing for understanding—not just functionality—especially when time and trust are limited.

My impact came not from execution, but from helping the team and stakeholders see the problem clearly enough to act.
